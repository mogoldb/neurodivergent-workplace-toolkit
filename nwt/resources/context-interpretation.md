# Context Interpretation Rules

Guidelines for decoding implicit meaning, workplace subtext, and vague language — common challenges for neurodivergent professionals who tend toward literal interpretation.

---

## Why This Is Hard

Neurotypical workplace communication is full of indirection. People soften requests, hedge deadlines, and embed asks inside casual-sounding messages. This isn't deceptive — it's a social norm. But it creates real ambiguity for people who process language more literally.

This guide translates common patterns.

---

## Timeline Phrases — What They Actually Mean

| What They Said | What They Usually Mean |
|----------------|------------------------|
| "When you get a chance" | Within 1-2 business days. If the team is blocked on you, it's urgent. |
| "No rush" | There IS a deadline, but they're being polite. Check context for clues. |
| "Soon" | Within the current sprint or week. |
| "At your earliest convenience" | As soon as possible, phrased politely. |
| "Eventually" | Lower priority, but not forgotten. Expect follow-up. |
| "Before our next meeting" | Firm deadline — that meeting IS the deadline. |
| "By end of week" | Friday COB in their timezone. |
| "ASAP" | Genuinely urgent. Drop other things if possible. |

**Rule of thumb:** When in doubt, treat the deadline as shorter rather than longer, and confirm: "Happy to — what's your timeline?"

## Request Phrases — What They're Really Asking

| What They Said | What They're Really Asking |
|----------------|---------------------------|
| "Thoughts?" | Wants specific feedback, or permission to proceed. Give a real answer. |
| "Can you take a look?" | Wants review, feedback, or approval — often all three. |
| "Just checking in" | Needs a status update. May be gentle escalation if overdue. |
| "Let me know" | Wants a response, even if the answer is "not yet" or "working on it." |
| "What do you think?" | Usually wants your opinion or sign-off, not just "looks good." |
| "Can you help me with X?" | Direct ask — they know what they need. |
| "We should talk about X" | They have a concern. Don't avoid it — schedule the conversation. |
| "I wanted to loop you in" | You're now on the hook for awareness; may soon be on the hook for action. |

## Status and Escalation Patterns

**"How's it going?"** — Depending on context:
- Casual / relationship building: no action needed
- From a manager before a deadline: status check, answer with progress and ETA
- Recurring: may be gentle escalation, worth asking "Do you need something specific?"

**"Following up on my earlier message"** — Escalation. They didn't get what they needed. Respond promptly.

**"Wanted to make sure this didn't fall through the cracks"** — Same as above. They're being diplomatic about the fact that they're following up.

**"Per my last email"** — Frustration. They feel ignored. Address the original ask directly.

---

## Common Communication Patterns

**Polite urgent request:** Soft language, hard deadline. "When you get a chance" said to someone who is a blocker for a deadline. Read the context, not just the words.

**Seeking approval disguised as seeking input:** "Thoughts?" often means "Can I proceed?" A non-response or "looks fine to me" is the green light they want.

**Checking if you're blocked on them:** "Just seeing if you have any questions" from someone you're working with often means "I want to make sure I'm not blocking you."

**Hedged criticism:** "I wonder if there might be another approach..." means "I think there's a problem with this approach." Take it seriously.

**Relationship maintenance:** "Hope you're doing well!" at the start of a message is social lubrication. It doesn't require a detailed personal response — "Thanks, you too!" is fine before getting to business.

---

## What to Do When You're Unsure

Asking for clarity is always better than guessing wrong. Use framing like:
- "Just want to make sure I understand — are you asking me to X?"
- "Happy to help — what's your timeline on this?"
- "To confirm: you need X by Y date, right?"

This is not a sign of incompetence. It is professional, efficient, and saves everyone time.
